Udupi Ramachandra Rao

Indian Space scientist

Here's a timeline highlighting key events and achievements in the life of Udupi Ramachandra Rao

  • 10th March 1932 - Udupi Ramachandra Rao was born in Adamaru, Karnataka, India.
  • 1952 - Graduated with a B.Sc. degree from Madras University, Chennai.
  • 1953 - Completed his M.Sc. degree in Physics from Banaras Hindu University, Varanasi.
  • 1960 - Earned his Ph.D. in Cosmic Ray Physics from Physical Research Laboratory (PRL), Ahmedabad.
  • 1963 - Joined the Department of Space (India) as a scientist.
  • 1972 - Became the Project Director for India's first satellite, Aryabhata.
  • 1975 - Aryabhata, India's first satellite, was successfully launched under Rao's leadership.
  • 1984 - U.R. Rao was appointed as the Chairman of the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).
  • 1985 - Initiated the INSAT (Indian National Satellite System) program, aimed at providing telecommunications, broadcasting, and meteorological services in India.
  • 1988 - Launched India's first dedicated meteorological satellite, INSAT-1A.
  • 1993 - Rao played a crucial role in the development and launch of the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV), a reliable and cost-effective launch vehicle for ISRO.
  • 2003 - Led the establishment of the Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology (IIST), the first space university in India.
  • 2005 - Received the prestigious International Astronautical Federation (IAF) Hall of Fame Award.
  • 2006 - Elected as the President of the International Astronautical Federation (IAF).
  • 2013 - Awarded the Padma Vibhushan, India's second-highest civilian award, for his contributions to space research.
  • 2016 - U.R. Rao passed away on 24th July, leaving behind a remarkable legacy in Indian space research and development.
    • Throughout his career, Udupi Ramachandra Rao played a pivotal role in shaping India's space program, leading numerous satellite missions and establishing ISRO as a globally recognized space agency. His contributions to the field of space research and his efforts in making space technology accessible to all have left an indelible mark on India's scientific community.
